The model & assumptions

Launch: top of the Harrison Square parking garage, ~55 ft above street level, ground ~790 ft ASL. This year's show is ~4,900 lb of shells.

Burst altitude: pyrotechnicians use ~100 ft per inch of shell diameter. A municipal show like this runs roughly 3″–8″ shells, so bursts sit about 300–900 ft above the garage (≈1,100–1,750 ft ASL). The model treats that band as the canopy and ~1,450 ft ASL as the marquee height.

Towers: the downtown cluster (Anthony Wayne Building, Lincoln Tower, PNC, the 442‑ft I&M Power Center) sits just northeast of the launch. If it falls between you and the garage, the low bursts hide and only the high shells clear. That's why south, west, and riverfront beat due‑northeast.

Trees: blocking is angular, atan(height / distance). A tall tree right across the street can erase a show a mile off, while distance barely dents it. In July's full leaf, an unbroken tree line is modeled as a wall up to ~3° (open) / ~14° (scattered) / ~32° (leafy) above the horizon; if that beats the canopy's elevation angle from your spot, you need an opening aimed at the launch.

This is a reasoning prototype, not survey data. Coordinates, tower heights, and the tree presets are approximate — no per‑building or per‑canopy data yet. Good for ranking spots, not for promising a rooftop clears by 3°.
